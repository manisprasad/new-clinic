Signs and Symptoms of Milk Allergy

The signs and symptoms of a milk allergy occur within minutes to hours after consuming milk or milk products. The allergic reaction to milk varies from one individual to other and even in the same person at different times. The most common signs and symptoms that arise as an immediate response after milk consumption include hives, swelling, wheezing and vomiting.
There are other symptoms that take time to develop in milk allergy. These late features include loose stool, colic, abdominal cramps, loose stool with blood especially in infants, skin rash, runny nose, and watery eyes. Sometimes a milk allergy can lead to a severe reaction known as anaphylaxis where the airways become narrow and breathing difficulty arises, causing the body to go into shock. It is a life-threatening condition that needs to be addressed immediately under the conventional mode of treatment.
